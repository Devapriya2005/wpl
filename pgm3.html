A village That Feels Like Home

Kumbalam is a village located in Kanayannur taluk of Ernakulam district in Kerala, India. Nestled along the serene shores of Vembanad Lake, Kumbalam is a vibrant village that beautifully blends tradition and convenience. Rich in heritage, the village is home to numerous temples and churches, reflecting its cultural diversity. Food lovers will delight in the many local food spots offering authentic flavors. With easy access via well-connected roads and transport facilities, Kumbalam is not only a peaceful retreat but also a welcoming gateway to explore the natural beauty and community spirit of the region.

History Of Kumbalam

Historically, the village was part of the thriving trade routes of Kerala, connecting inland communities with the coastal ports. Over the centuries, it has developed as a peaceful settlement, known for its temples and churches, which reflect the harmonious coexistence of different faiths. Today, it stands as a vibrant community that honors its cultural and historical roots while embracing modern life.

Over the centuries, Kumbalam became known for its religious diversity and harmony. Numerous temples and churches were established, many of which are centuries old, serving as centers of community life and festivals. The architecture and traditions of these religious sites reflect the blending of local Kerala culture with influences from traders, missionaries, and settlers who passed through the region.

The village has also witnessed the evolution of modern infrastructure, including improved roads and water transport, which connected it more closely with nearby towns and cities. Despite these changes, Kumbalam has retained its village charm, with scenic views of Vembanad Lake, traditional homes, and bustling local food spots that give visitors a glimpse of authentic village life.

Today it is offering both a peaceful retreat and a vibrant community rich in culture, religion, and natural beauty.

How to Get Here..?

  1. By Train

    2. Kumbalam is conveniently accessible by train via the Kumbalam Railway Station. The station is well-connected to major cities and towns, making it easy for visitors to reach the village. From the station, the village center is just a short ride away.

  2. By water Metro

    3. Kumbalam is also accessible via the Water Metro from Vembanad Lake, offering a scenic and convenient route to the village. Regular services connect the village to nearby areas, making it a unique and enjoyable travel option for visitors.

  3. By Bus

    4. Kumbalam is well-connected by bus, with frequent services from nearby towns and cities. Bus stops are conveniently located near the village center, making it easy for visitors to reach popular spots and local attractions
